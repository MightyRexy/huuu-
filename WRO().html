    What Is WRO About

    WRO (World Robot Olympiad) is an international robotics competition where students design, build and program robots to complete
    specific challenges.It encourages creativity problem-solving, and teamwork while prmoting STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering,
    and Mathematics) education.

    One of the key benefits of WRO is that it enhances critical thinking and problem-solving skills. Students
    must anaylze challenges, experiment with different solutions, and refine their designs through trail and error. Additionally, WRO
    promotes teamwork, as participants work in groups to strategize and optimize their robot's performance.

    Beyond technical skills, WRO prepares students for future careers in engineering, AI, and automation. By applying coding and robicts
    knowledge in a competitive setting, students gain hands-on experience that can lead to their future opportunities. Ultimately, robotics
    competitions inspire young minds to pursue careers in science and technology, shaping the innovative of tomorrow.

    Main Aspects of WRO:

    1. Competition Categories:

    • RoboMission-Autonomous robots complete tasks on a game field.
    • Future Innovators – Teams create innovative robotics projects to solve real-world problems.
    • RoboSports – Two robots compete in a game-based challenge (like robotic soccer).
    • Future Engineers – Advanced robotics projects focusing on engineering and AI.

    2. Age groups

    • Elementary (up to 12 years old)
    • Junior (13-15 years old))
    • Senior (16-19 years old)

    3. Technology Used

    • Robots are usually built using LEGO Mindstorms, SPIKE Prime, Arduino, or Raspberry Pi.
    • Programming languages include Python, C++, or Scratch-based block coding.

    4. Global Competition

    • Teams compete in national rounds first. Winners move to the international WRO finals.
